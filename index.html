Biryani

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves)
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. :Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. :Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, mint leaves, coriander leaves, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. :Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. :Add whole spices and fry for a minute.
  5. :Add whole spices and fry for a minute.
  6. :Add marinated chicken pieces and cook until the chicken is half done.
  7. :Add soaked rice and mix well. Add water (1:2 ratio of rice to water).
  8. :Cover and cook until the rice is done and the chicken is cooked through.
  9. :Garnish with fried onions and chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot.

Pizza

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 1 package (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • teaspoon granulated sugar
  • cup warm water (110°F/45°C)
  • 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • teaspoon salt
  • tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a dough forms.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.
  6. Punch down the dough and divide it into two equal portions. Roll out each portion into a round shape, about 12 inches in diameter.
  7. Place the rolled-out dough onto a pizza peel or lightly greased baking sheet.
  8. Spread tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  9. Add your desired toppings such as pepperoni, bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, and fresh basil leaves.
  10. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the toppings.
  11. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the toppings.
  12. Bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and melted.
  13. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Haleem

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 3-4 hour

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat grains (broken wheat) 1/2 cup barley 1/2 cup split Bengal gram (chana dal) 1/2 cup split red lentils (masoor dal) 1/2 cup split pigeon peas (toor dal) 500 grams boneless mutton or chicken, cut into small pieces 1 large onion, thinly sliced 4-5 cloves garlic, minced 1-inch piece of ginger, grated 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste) 2 tomatoes, chopped 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) 2 teaspoons garam masala powder Salt to taste Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped, for garnish Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

    1. Rinse the whole wheat grains, barley, and lentils under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak them in water for at least 2 hours or overnight.
    2. In a large pot or pressure cooker, add the soaked grains and lentils along with 6-7 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the grains and lentils are cooked and tender. This may take about 1-2 hours, depending on the type of grains used.
    3. Once the grains and lentils are cooked, use an immersion blender or a potato masher to mash them to a thick, porridge-like consistency. Set aside.
    4. In another large pot or skillet, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown and caramelized.
    5. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies to the pot. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes until aromatic.
    6. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
    7. Add the mutton or chicken pieces to the pot along with turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt to taste. Cook until the meat is browned on all sides.
    8. Add the mashed grains and lentils to the pot and mix well with the meat and spices.
    9. Add enough water to achieve the desired consistency. The haleem should have a thick, stew-like consistency. Simmer for another 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors are well combined and the haleem reaches the desired thickness.
    10. Sprinkle garam masala powder over the haleem and mix well. Adjust the seasoning if necessary.
    11. Serve hot garnished with fresh cilantro leaves and lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy your delicious haleem!

chicken 65

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 2 hour

Ingredients

  • 500 grams boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 egg
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Curry leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt to taste. Mix well to form a smooth paste.
  3. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix until the chicken is well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  4. Dip each marinated chicken piece in the beaten egg, then coat it with the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  5. Carefully drop the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil, in batches, and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, about 5-6 minutes.
  6. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried chicken pieces from the oil and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. In a separate pan, heat a little oil and add curry leaves and sliced green chilies. Fry for a few seconds until fragrant.
  8. Garnish the fried chicken with the fried curry leaves and green chilies.
  9. Serve hot as an appetizer or snack, garnished with lemon wedges and onion rings. Enjoy your homemade Chicken 65! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences
